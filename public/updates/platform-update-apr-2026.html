What's new · April 2026

A substantial update for the way you actually train

Your coach got a lot more useful on your phone, your workouts got more shareable, and our first athlete-centered white paper is live. We also tightened how closely each coach stays in its lane — in a good way.

Bigger picture: we're rolling out the ability to chat with your coach from anywhere in the app, without leaving the page you're on. It's live on the Training Grounds and Workout Details pages today, with more pages close behind — so you can ask a question, log a set, or talk through how a workout felt right where you already are, instead of hopping over to a dedicated chat page.

A quick walk through what shipped over roughly the last four weeks.

Platform Update — April 2026

Training Grounds, now with an immersive mobile coach chat. The first public white paper from a real athlete. Richer workout surfaces, sharper safety, and a lot of small polish.

Every change below traces back to how people are actually using NeonPanda — logging from their phone, asking their coach follow-up questions mid-session, sharing a hard workout with a training partner. Thank you for shaping it.

New

Training Grounds: talk to your coach from anywhere

A floating action button on Training Grounds now opens a full coach chat right on top of the page you're already on — no context switch, no lost train of thought.

  • Full composer: text, photos, and file attachments — log a workout, ask a question, or drop a form video without leaving the screen you're on.
  • Conversation picker: a compact combobox (same feel as the Training Pulse exercise selector) to jump between threads in one tap.
  • Quick actions: start a new chat, pop the current thread into the full-page view, or open every conversation — each with tooltips where it counts.
  • Helpful empty state: curated tip cards when a thread is brand new, so first messages are easier to start.
  • Mobile fixes that matter: history stack, scroll-to-bottom on thread switch, and stable close behavior when reopening the drawer.

Open Training Grounds on your phone, tap the chat button, and you'll see what we mean.

Share

Share any workout as a clean 9:16 card

Finished a workout you want to send to a training partner, post to a story, or tuck into your own season journal? Every completed workout now has a shareable 1080×1920 branded card — generated from the data, not a screenshot.

  • AI-written workout summary as a quote block for at-a-glance context.
  • Discipline-aware 2×2 metrics (duration, volume, exercises, sets) plus a structured movements section.
  • RPE and Intensity shown as gradient bars so effort reads instantly.
  • Running cards show distance and pace segments; cycling cards show ride type, distance, speed, elevation, and surface.
  • Web Share on capable mobile browsers — post straight to the app of your choice.

From any completed workout, open the three-dot menu on the card or the action bar on the detail page.

Upgraded

Edit your workouts by just talking

Logged a workout and realized you forgot a set, or called an exercise by the wrong name? Tell your coach in chat — the agent now takes conversational edits on already-logged workouts, so the fix happens where you already are instead of in a form.

Expanded

Cycling joins the lineup, plus richer workout surfaces

Cycling is now a first-class discipline with its own extraction pipeline and display — power zones, elevation, speed, cadence, heart rate, distance, and TrainerRoad-style ride structure. Along the way, we upgraded a lot of what you already see:

  • Up to 8 exercises shown before the "+N more" indicator on workout cards (was 6).
  • Running, circuit, and functional displays got cleaner layouts for rounds and segments.
  • Units come from your workout data — no more hardcoded miles when you logged in kilometers.
  • Discipline detection and metrics integrity fixes so logs read the way they were intended.
Smarter

Your coach keeps up with longer conversations

We've been quietly extending how much context your coach actually carries between messages — without overwhelming the conversation or slowing things down.

  • Rolling conversation summary beyond the active 60-message window, so longer threads stay coherent.
  • Four-tier memory — goals, living profile, lifecycle, and emotional context — feeding prompts and summaries.
  • Images in logging flow through the chat path (within sensible limits) for quicker capture.
  • Prospective-memory filter hardened so casual mentions don't get mistaken for platform-support asks.

Net effect: longer back-and-forths that keep their thread, without surprise memory drops in the middle of a session.

Smarter boundaries

Your coach isn't auditioning for side gigs anymore

We tightened how closely each coach sticks to the rules and role you designed. The personality you picked is now much less likely to wander off into a surprise hobby lecture on a subject they were never built to cover.

  • Think of it as better stage directions: fewer off-script monologues, more focus on the training conversation you actually showed up for.
  • Under the hood: the training directive, conversation summary, profile, and emotional context are all sanitized and wrapped before they reach the model — so the guardrails you set are the ones that stick.

Same coach voice you picked. Better at staying in its lane.
