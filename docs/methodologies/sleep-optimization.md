# Sleep Optimization

## Overview

Sleep Optimization is a comprehensive methodology focused on maximizing sleep quality, duration, and recovery through evidence-based sleep hygiene practices, environmental modifications, and lifestyle interventions. This approach recognizes sleep as the foundation of athletic performance, recovery, and overall health, emphasizing systematic strategies to enhance all aspects of sleep architecture and restorative processes.

## Creator/Origin

Sleep Optimization as a systematic methodology emerged from collective research by sleep scientists, sports scientists, and performance specialists over decades of study. The approach is rooted in sleep science research, circadian rhythm studies, and performance optimization work from institutions worldwide.

The philosophy centers on sleep as the most critical recovery and performance enhancement tool available to athletes. Key contributors include Matthew Walker (sleep researcher), Shona Halson (sports science), and Charles Samuels (sport sleep specialist), whose work has demonstrated the profound impact of sleep on athletic performance, recovery, and health.

## WORKOUT TEMPLATES

**Note**: For lifestyle methodologies like Sleep Optimization, "Workout Templates" are replaced with Protocol Templates that integrate with training.

**Protocol Structure & Integration:**

- **Sleep Priority**: 8-10 hours for athletes in heavy training
- **Training Timing**: Last workout 4+ hours before bed
- **Recovery Days**: Allow extra sleep opportunity
- **Competition**: Maintain schedule, arrive early to adapt

**Example Daily Protocol:**

```
Morning (6:00-7:00 AM):
- Wake at consistent time (within 30 min daily)
- Bright light exposure within 30 min of waking
- Light movement/stretching

Pre-Sleep (8:00-10:00 PM):
- Dim lights 2 hours before bed
- No screens 1 hour before bed
- Cool bedroom (65-68°F / 18-20°C)
- Complete darkness for sleep

Sleep Window (10:00 PM - 6:00 AM):
- 8-hour opportunity
- Consistent timing 7 days/week
```

**Training Integration:**

```
Heavy Training Days:
- Add 30-60 min sleep opportunity
- Prioritize post-workout nap (20-30 min)
- Earlier bedtime if morning training planned

Competition Week:
- Arrive 2-3 days early for time zone changes
- Maintain home sleep schedule initially
- Gradually shift to competition timing
```
