# Athletic Performance Training

## Overview

Athletic Performance Training is a comprehensive methodology focused on developing sport-specific physical qualities, movement skills, and performance capabilities required for competitive athletics. This system emphasizes the integration of strength, power, speed, agility, endurance, and sport-specific skills through periodized training programs designed to optimize athletic performance.

## Creator/Origin

- **Creator**: Collective development by sports scientists, strength coaches, and performance specialists
- **Origin**: Evolution from traditional strength training and sport science research
- **Background**: Based on principles of sports science, biomechanics, and performance optimization
- **Philosophy**: Systematic development of physical qualities specific to athletic performance demands

## Key Principles

### 1. Sport-Specific Development

- Training that mimics sport demands
- Movement patterns specific to athletic requirements
- Energy system development matching sport needs
- Skill transfer to competitive performance

### 2. Periodization

- Systematic planning of training phases
- Progressive overload and adaptation
- Peak performance timing
- Recovery and regeneration integration

### 3. Multi-Component Fitness

- Strength, power, speed, and endurance integration
- Agility, coordination, and balance development
- Flexibility and mobility optimization
- Mental and cognitive skill enhancement

### 4. Individual Adaptation

- Customized programming based on sport demands
- Individual strengths and weaknesses assessment
- Position-specific requirements
- Injury history and risk factors consideration

### 5. Performance Monitoring

- Objective performance measurement
- Training load monitoring
- Recovery and adaptation tracking
- Data-driven program adjustments

## Programming Structure

### Daily Structure

- **Warm-up**: Dynamic preparation and activation (15-20 minutes)
- **Skill/Speed**: Technical and speed development (20-30 minutes)
- **Strength/Power**: Force and power development (30-40 minutes)
- **Conditioning**: Energy system development (15-25 minutes)
- **Cool-down**: Recovery and regeneration (15-20 minutes)

### Weekly Programming

- **Monday**: Maximum strength and power development
- **Tuesday**: Speed and agility training
- **Wednesday**: Sport-specific skills and conditioning
- **Thursday**: Strength endurance and recovery
- **Friday**: Power and explosive movement training
- **Saturday**: Competition simulation or testing
- **Sunday**: Active recovery and regeneration

### Monthly Cycles

- **Week 1-2**: Volume accumulation and skill development
- **Week 3**: Intensity increase and performance focus
- **Week 4**: Recovery and adaptation consolidation

### Seasonal Periodization

- **Off-season**: General preparation and base building
- **Pre-season**: Specific preparation and skill development
- **In-season**: Competition performance and maintenance
- **Post-season**: Recovery and regeneration

## WORKOUT TEMPLATES

The following templates demonstrate how Athletic Performance Training principles can be applied to different phases and athlete needs. Each template should be customized based on sport demands, individual assessment, and competition schedule.

### GPP Phase (General Preparation) - Field Sport Athlete

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks
**Frequency**: 5-6 sessions/week
**Focus**: Broad fitness foundation and movement quality

**Day 1 - Lower Body Strength**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10-15 minutes
- Squat variation: 4×6-8 @ 70-80%
- Romanian deadlift: 3×8-10
- Split squat: 3×8-10 each leg
- Leg press: 3×10-12
- Core circuit: 3 rounds

**Day 2 - Upper Body Strength**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10-15 minutes
- Bench press: 4×6-8 @ 70-80%
- Bent-over row: 4×6-8
- Overhead press: 3×8-10
- Pull-ups: 3×max
- Accessory work: 3 sets each

**Day 3 - Speed and Agility**

- Dynamic warm-up: 15 minutes
- Sprint drills: 4-6 sets
- Agility ladder: 3 sets
- Change of direction: 4-6 reps
- Conditioning: 15-20 minutes

**Day 4 - Power Development**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10-15 minutes
- Box jumps: 4×5
- Medicine ball throws: 4×6
- Hang clean: 4×4 @ 60-70%
- Plyometric circuit: 3 rounds

**Day 5 - Conditioning and Recovery**

- Aerobic conditioning: 30-40 minutes
- Mobility work: 20 minutes
- Sport-specific skill work

### SPP Phase (Specific Preparation) - Sprint Athlete

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks
**Frequency**: 5-6 sessions/week
**Focus**: Speed development and power optimization

**Day 1 - Max Velocity Development**

- Extended warm-up: 20-25 minutes
- Wicket drills: 3-4 sets
- Flying 30m sprints: 4-6 @ 95-100%
- Recovery: 3-4 minutes between reps
- Speed-strength plyometrics: 3 sets
- Cool-down and flexibility

**Day 2 - Lower Body Power**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10-15 minutes
- Clean pulls: 4×3 @ 80-90%
- Back squat: 4×3-5 @ 80-85%
- Single-leg RDL: 3×6 each
- Hip thrust: 3×8
- Core anti-rotation: 3 sets

**Day 3 - Acceleration Development**

- Extended warm-up: 20-25 minutes
- Block starts: 6-8 @ 100%
- 30m accelerations: 4-6 @ 95-100%
- Sled pushes: 4×20m
- Recovery: 4-5 minutes between reps
- Cool-down

**Day 4 - Upper Body and Core**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10-15 minutes
- Incline bench: 4×4-6 @ 80%
- Weighted pull-ups: 4×4-6
- Push press: 3×5
- Medicine ball throws: 4×6
- Core stability: 3 sets

**Day 5 - Speed Endurance**

- Extended warm-up: 20-25 minutes
- Special endurance runs: 3-4×150m @ 90-95%
- Recovery: 8-10 minutes between reps
- Cool-down and flexibility

### Competition Phase - Team Sport Athlete

**Duration**: Ongoing during season
**Frequency**: 2-3 strength sessions/week (maintenance)
**Focus**: Maintain fitness while peaking for competition

**Day 1 - Lower Body Maintenance**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10 minutes
- Squat: 3×4-5 @ 80-85%
- RDL: 3×5-6
- Split squat: 2×6 each leg
- Explosive jumps: 3×4
- Core: 2 sets

**Day 2 - Upper Body Maintenance**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10 minutes
- Bench press: 3×4-5 @ 80-85%
- Pull-ups: 3×6-8
- DB row: 3×8 each
- Rotator cuff: 2 sets each
- Core: 2 sets

**Recovery Session**

- Light aerobic: 20 minutes
- Mobility work: 20 minutes
- Foam rolling: 10 minutes
- Sport-specific light practice

### Transition/Recovery Week Template

**Duration**: 1 week
**Frequency**: 3-4 sessions
**Focus**: Active recovery and regeneration

**Day 1 - Light Movement**

- Pool session: 30 minutes
- Mobility work: 20 minutes
- Light stretching

**Day 2 - Active Recovery**

- Cycling or swimming: 30 minutes @ 50-60% effort
- Foam rolling: 15 minutes
- Flexibility work: 15 minutes

**Day 3 - Movement Prep**

- Bodyweight circuits: 3 rounds (light)
- Core stability: 3 sets
- Balance work: 15 minutes
- Cool-down

**Day 4 - Sport-Specific Light**

- Light skill practice: 30 minutes
- Recovery modalities as needed
- Mental preparation

### Youth Athlete Development Template

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Frequency**: 3-4 sessions/week
**Focus**: Movement literacy and athletic foundation

**Day 1 - Fundamental Movements**

- Dynamic warm-up: 15 minutes (movement games)
- Squat progressions: 3×10 bodyweight
- Push-up progressions: 3×8-12
- Jumping and landing: 4×5
- Crawling patterns: 3×20m
- Movement games: 10 minutes

**Day 2 - Speed and Coordination**

- Dynamic warm-up: 10 minutes
- Sprint mechanics: 4-6 drills
- Agility games: 15 minutes
- Coordination drills: 4 sets
- Fun relay races

**Day 3 - Strength and Stability**

- Animal walks warm-up: 10 minutes
- Goblet squats: 3×10
- Rows (band or cable): 3×10
- Core exercises: 3 sets
- Balance challenges: 10 minutes
- Movement games: 10 minutes

### Notes for All Templates

- **Individualization**: Adjust volumes and intensities based on individual response and sport demands
- **Monitoring**: Track training load, recovery markers, and performance metrics
- **Progression**: Advance through phases systematically based on assessment results
- **Recovery**: Ensure adequate rest between high-intensity sessions
- **Nutrition**: Support training with appropriate fueling strategies
- **Communication**: Coordinate with sport coaches and medical staff
