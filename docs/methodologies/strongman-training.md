# Strongman Training

## Overview

Strongman Training is a comprehensive methodology focused on developing maximal strength, power, and functional capacity through the use of unconventional implements and movements. This system emphasizes real-world strength applications, grip strength, and the ability to move heavy, awkward objects through various planes of motion, preparing athletes for strongman competitions and functional strength demands.

## Creator/Origin

- **Creator**: Collective development from strongman competitors and strength coaches
- **Origin**: Evolution from traditional strongman competitions and Highland Games
- **Background**: Based on functional strength demands and competition requirements
- **Philosophy**: Developing strength that translates to real-world applications and competition success

## Key Principles

### 1. Functional Strength Development

- Real-world strength applications
- Multi-planar movement patterns
- Grip strength and endurance
- Strength in awkward positions and ranges of motion

### 2. Implement Training

- Specialized strongman equipment utilization
- Atlas stones, logs, yokes, and farmer's walks
- Unconventional loading patterns
- Adaptation to varying implement weights and shapes

### 3. Maximal Strength Focus

- Development of absolute strength
- Progressive overload with heavy implements
- Strength endurance combinations
- Power development through explosive movements

### 4. Competition Preparation

- Event-specific training
- Competition simulation
- Mental preparation for competition
- Strategy development for events

### 5. Work Capacity Development

- Strength endurance integration
- Conditioning through strength training
- Recovery between high-intensity efforts
- Sustained performance capability

## Programming Structure

### Daily Structure

- **Warm-up**: Joint mobility and movement preparation (15-20 minutes)
- **Main Training**: Strongman events and strength work (60-90 minutes)
- **Accessory Work**: Weakness addressing and support training (20-30 minutes)
- **Cool-down**: Recovery and mobility work (15-20 minutes)

### Weekly Programming

- **Monday**: Pressing events and overhead strength
- **Tuesday**: Deadlift variations and pulling strength
- **Wednesday**: Carrying and loading events
- **Thursday**: Squat variations and leg strength
- **Friday**: Competition simulation and testing
- **Saturday**: Accessory work and conditioning
- **Sunday**: Rest or light recovery work

### Monthly Cycles

- **Week 1-2**: Volume accumulation and technique development
- **Week 3**: Intensity increase and competition preparation
- **Week 4**: Deload and recovery emphasis

### Seasonal Periodization

- **Off-season**: Base strength building and technique development
- **Pre-competition**: Event-specific training and peaking
- **Competition**: Performance optimization and recovery
- **Post-competition**: Recovery and base rebuilding

## WORKOUT TEMPLATES

The following templates demonstrate how strongman training principles can be applied to different training phases and experience levels. Each template should be adjusted based on equipment availability, competition schedule, and individual strengths and weaknesses.

### Off-Season Strength Block - 4-Day Split

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Frequency**: 4 sessions/week
**Focus**: Building maximal strength foundation

**Day 1 - Max Effort Lower**

- Squat variation (rotate weekly): Work up to 1-3RM
  - Week 1: Back squat
  - Week 2: Front squat
  - Week 3: Safety bar squat
  - Week 4: Box squat
- Deficit deadlift: 4×3-5 @ 70-80%
- Good mornings: 3×8-10
- Reverse hypers: 3×12-15
- Ab wheel: 3×10-15

**Day 2 - Max Effort Upper**

- Press variation (rotate weekly): Work up to 1-3RM
  - Week 1: Log press
  - Week 2: Strict overhead press
  - Week 3: Incline bench
  - Week 4: Push press
- Dumbbell rows: 4×8-10 each
- Weighted dips: 3×8-10
- Face pulls: 4×15-20
- Hammer curls: 3×12-15

**Day 3 - Dynamic Lower/Events**

- Speed deadlifts: 8×2 @ 50-60% + bands
- Farmer's walk: 4×50 feet (moderate weight)
- Yoke walk: 4×50 feet (moderate weight)
- Sled drags: 4×100 feet
- Core work: 3 sets

**Day 4 - Repetition Upper/Accessories**

- Close-grip bench: 4×8-10
- Lat pulldowns: 4×10-12
- Overhead tricep work: 3×12-15
- Barbell curls: 3×10-12
- Lateral raises: 3×15-20
- Grip work: 3 sets

### Competition Prep Block - 4-Day Split

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks leading to competition
**Frequency**: 4 sessions/week
**Focus**: Event-specific training and peaking

**Day 1 - Deadlift/Posterior Chain**

- Competition deadlift: Work up to heavy singles/doubles
- Stiff-leg deadlifts: 3×6-8
- Barbell rows: 4×6-8
- Glute-ham raises: 3×8-10
- Core work: 3 sets

**Day 2 - Overhead/Push**

- Competition overhead event: Work up to heavy singles
- Push press: 3×5
- Incline dumbbell press: 3×8-10
- Tricep extensions: 3×12-15
- Rear delt work: 3×15-20

**Day 3 - Events Day (Rotate)**

- Weeks 1-4: Focus on 2-3 events (lighter, technique)
- Weeks 5-8: Full event simulation (moderate-heavy)
- Weeks 9-12: Competition weight events (reduced volume)

Example rotation:

- Farmer's walk: 3×60 feet
- Atlas stones: 5×1-3 reps
- Sandbag carry: 3×100 feet
- Tire flip: 5×3-5 flips

**Day 4 - Squat/Assistance**

- Front squat or safety bar squat: 4×4-6
- Leg press: 3×10-12
- Walking lunges: 3×20 steps
- Leg curls: 3×12-15
- Calves: 4×15-20

### Beginner Strongman Introduction

**Duration**: 12-16 weeks
**Frequency**: 3 sessions/week
**Focus**: Building base strength and learning events

**Day 1 - Lower Body**

- Back squat: 4×6-8
- Romanian deadlift: 3×8-10
- Leg press: 3×10-12
- Glute bridges: 3×12-15
- Farmer's walk (light): 4×40 feet
- Planks: 3×45 sec

**Day 2 - Upper Body**

- Overhead press: 4×6-8
- Barbell rows: 4×6-8
- Dumbbell bench press: 3×8-10
- Lat pulldowns: 3×10-12
- Dumbbell curls: 3×10-12
- Tricep pushdowns: 3×10-12

**Day 3 - Deadlift/Events Intro**

- Conventional deadlift: 4×5
- Front squats: 3×6-8
- Sandbag carries (light): 4×40 feet
- Tire flips (light): 4×5 flips
- Grip work: 3 sets

**Progression Notes:**

- Add weight when all reps completed with good form
- Introduce new events every 3-4 weeks
- Focus on technique before weight
- Rest 2-3 minutes between heavy sets

### In-Season Maintenance

**Duration**: Competition season
**Frequency**: 2-3 sessions/week
**Focus**: Maintaining strength while recovering between competitions

**Day 1 - Lower Maintenance**

- Squat variation: 3×4-5 @ 80-85%
- Deficit deadlift: 3×3-5 @ 70-75%
- Good mornings: 3×8
- Light event work: 20-30 min
- Core: 2 sets

**Day 2 - Upper Maintenance**

- Press variation: 3×4-5 @ 80-85%
- Rows: 3×8
- Accessory work: 2 sets each
- Grip maintenance: 2 sets

**Day 3 (Optional) - Light Events/Recovery**

- Light technique work on weak events
- Conditioning: 15-20 min
- Mobility work: 15-20 min

### Event-Specific Training Day

**Duration**: 1 session
**Focus**: Competition simulation and event practice

**Warm-Up (15-20 min)**

- General: 5 min cardio
- Dynamic stretching
- Event-specific warm-up sets

**Event Rotation (60-90 min)**
Format: 3-4 events, rotate through with competition rest periods

Example Day 1:

1. Log clean and press: 5 sets of singles (building)
2. Farmer's walk: 4×60 feet (building weight)
3. Atlas stones to platform: 5 sets of 1-3 reps

Example Day 2:

1. Axle deadlift: Work up to max
2. Yoke walk: 4×50 feet
3. Sandbag load: 5 sets

Example Day 3:

1. Circus dumbbell: 5 sets of singles
2. Tire flip: 5×3-5 flips
3. Car deadlift simulation: 4×3-5

**Cool-Down (10-15 min)**

- Light walking
- Stretching
- Foam rolling

### Grip-Focused Training Block

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (can be run concurrently)
**Frequency**: 3 dedicated grip sessions/week
**Focus**: Developing competition-level grip strength

**Session 1 - Support Grip**

- Heavy barbell holds: 3×max time
- Fat bar deadlifts: 3×5
- Farmer's walk (heavy): 4×40 feet
- Plate pinches: 3×30 sec

**Session 2 - Crushing Grip**

- Gripper work: 5×3-5 closes each hand
- Hub lifts: 3×max
- Thick bar rows: 3×8-10
- Wrist curls: 3×15

**Session 3 - Finger/Pinch Strength**

- Pinch block lifts: 4×1-3
- Finger curls: 3×15-20
- Sledgehammer levering: 3×8-10 each direction
- Band extensions: 3×20

### Notes for All Templates

- **Equipment Alternatives**: Many events can be simulated with standard gym equipment
- **Safety**: Always have spotters for max attempts; use proper implements
- **Recovery**: Strongman training is highly demanding; prioritize sleep and nutrition
- **Grip Care**: Maintain calluses; use chalk appropriately
- **Event Cycling**: Rotate events to prevent overuse and maintain variety
- **Deload**: Reduce volume/intensity every 4-6 weeks
- **Competition Prep**: Reduce training volume 7-10 days before competition
