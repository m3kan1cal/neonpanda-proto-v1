# Calisthenics/Bodyweight Training

## Overview

Calisthenics/Bodyweight Training is a comprehensive methodology that utilizes body weight as the primary resistance for developing strength, flexibility, coordination, and body control. This system emphasizes progressive skill development, movement mastery, and functional strength through gymnastic-inspired movements and exercises that require minimal equipment while maximizing physical development.

## Creator/Origin

Calisthenics has ancient origins in Greek military training and gymnastics, with the term derived from the Greek words "kalos" (beautiful) and "sthenos" (strength). Modern calisthenics has been shaped by gymnasts, martial artists, and fitness enthusiasts who have systematized progressive bodyweight training.

Contemporary figures like Al Kavadlo, the Barstarzz movement, and Gymnastic Bodies founder Christopher Sommer have popularized structured approaches to bodyweight strength and skill development.

## Programming Structure

- **Warm-up**: Joint mobility and movement preparation (15-20 minutes)
- **Skill Work**: Technical practice and progression (20-30 minutes)

- **Monday**: Upper body push movements and skills

### Disadvantages

#### Progression Challenges

- Slower strength gains compared to weighted training
- Difficult to quantify progress
- Plateau potential without proper progression
- May require creative exercise modifications

#### Skill Requirements

- Steep learning curve for advanced movements
- Requires patience and consistent practice
- Technical complexity of movements
- May be frustrating for some individuals

#### Limitations

- Limited lower body strength development
- Difficulty in targeting specific muscle groups
- May not suit all fitness goals
- Requires good baseline fitness for progression

#### Injury Risk

- Risk of overuse injuries from repetitive movements
- Potential for acute injury from advanced skills
- Requires proper warm-up and progression
- May exacerbate existing joint issues

## WORKOUT TEMPLATES

The following templates demonstrate how calisthenics principles can be applied to different training levels and goals. Each template should be adjusted based on current strength levels and progression needs.

### Beginner Foundation Program

**Duration**: 8-12 weeks
**Frequency**: 3 sessions/week (Full body)
**Focus**: Building baseline strength and movement quality

**Day 1 - Push Focus**

- Wall push-ups: 3×15-20
- Incline push-ups: 3×10-15
- Negative push-ups: 3×5-8
- Plank hold: 3×30 sec
- Dead bugs: 3×10 each side
- Bodyweight squats: 3×15-20
- Glute bridges: 3×15

**Day 2 - Pull Focus**

- Dead hangs: 3×15-30 sec
- Scapular pulls: 3×8-10
- Australian rows (high bar): 3×8-12
- Negative pull-ups: 3×3-5
- Hollow body holds: 3×15-20 sec
- Lunges: 3×10 each leg
- Single-leg glute bridges: 3×10 each

**Day 3 - Full Body**

- Push-ups (best progression): 3×max
- Rows (best progression): 3×max
- Squats: 3×15-20
- Pike push-ups: 3×5-10
- Assisted dips (if available): 3×5-8
- Core circuit: 3 rounds

### Intermediate Strength Program

**Duration**: 12-16 weeks
**Frequency**: 4 sessions/week (Upper/Lower split)
**Focus**: Building strength through progressive overload

**Upper Day 1 - Push Emphasis**

- Dips: 4×6-10
- Diamond push-ups: 4×8-12
- Pike push-ups: 4×6-10
- Archer push-ups: 3×5-8 each
- Pseudo planche leans: 3×10-15 sec
- Tricep extensions: 3×10-12
- L-sit progressions: 3×10-20 sec

**Upper Day 2 - Pull Emphasis**

- Pull-ups: 4×6-10
- Australian rows (feet elevated): 4×8-12
- Chin-ups: 3×6-10
- Archer rows: 3×5-8 each
- Bodyweight bicep curls (rings/bar): 3×10-12
- Hanging leg raises: 3×8-12
- Front lever progressions: 3×5-10 sec

**Lower Day 1 - Strength**

- Pistol squat progressions: 4×5-8 each leg
- Shrimp squat progressions: 4×5-8 each leg
- Bulgarian split squats: 3×10-12 each
- Nordic curl progressions: 3×5-8
- Calf raises: 4×15-20
- Glute bridges (single leg): 3×12-15 each

**Lower Day 2 - Volume**

- Jump squats: 4×8-10
- Walking lunges: 3×20 total
- Step-ups: 3×10-12 each
- Hip thrusts: 4×12-15
- Wall sit: 3×30-45 sec
- Mobility work: 15 min

### Advanced Skill-Focused Program

**Duration**: Ongoing
**Frequency**: 5-6 sessions/week
**Focus**: Skill acquisition and high-level strength

**Day 1 - Planche Work**

- Planche lean: 5×10-15 sec

**Day 2 - Front Lever Work**

- Front lever holds (best progression): 5×5-10 sec
- Ice cream makers: 3×5-8

**Day 3 - Lower Body Strength**

- Pistol squats: 5×5 each leg
- Shrimp squats: 4×5-8 each leg

- Nordic curls: 4×5-8

- Ring muscle-up work: 3×3-5
- Freestanding handstand practice: 10 min

- Pike push-ups: 3×8-12

**Day 5 - Back Lever and Skills**

- Back lever progressions: 5×5-10 sec

- Skin the cats: 3×5

### Minimal Equipment Home Workout

**Duration**: Ongoing
**Frequency**: 3-4 sessions/week
**Focus**: Building strength with no equipment

**Day 1 - Upper Push**

- Push-up variations: 4×max (various hand positions)
- Pike push-ups: 4×8-12
- Decline push-ups: 3×10-15
- Diamond push-ups: 3×10-15
- Plank variations: 3×30-45 sec
- Tricep dips (chair/step): 3×10-15

**Day 2 - Upper Pull (minimal equipment)**

- Towel rows (door): 4×10-15
- Isometric pulls (door frame): 4×10-15 sec
- Reverse plank: 3×30 sec
- Superman holds: 4×15-20
- Prone Y raises: 3×12-15
- Prone W raises: 3×12-15

**Day 3 - Lower Body**

- Squat variations: 4×15-20
- Lunges: 4×12 each leg
- Glute bridges: 4×15-20
- Single-leg deadlifts: 3×10 each leg
- Calf raises: 4×20
- Wall sit: 3×45-60 sec

**Day 4 - Full Body Circuit**
3-4 rounds:

- Burpees: 10 reps
- Push-ups: 15 reps
- Squats: 20 reps
- Mountain climbers: 20 each
- Plank: 30 sec
- Lunges: 10 each leg
  Rest: 2 min between rounds

### Flexibility/Mobility Focus Program

**Duration**: Ongoing (can be combined with strength work)
**Frequency**: Daily, 20-30 minutes
**Focus**: Improving range of motion and body control

**Morning Routine**

- Cat-cow stretches: 10 reps
- Thread the needle: 5 each side
- World's greatest stretch: 5 each side
- Deep squat hold: 2×1 min
- Pancake stretch: 2×1 min

**Pre-Workout Mobility**

- Wrist circles and stretches: 2 min
- Shoulder dislocates: 10-15 reps
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Leg swings: 10 each direction
- Thoracic rotations: 10 each side

**Post-Workout Flexibility**

- Pike stretch: 2×1 min
- Straddle stretch: 2×1 min
- Hip flexor stretch: 1 min each side
- Shoulder stretch: 1 min each
- Chest stretch: 1 min
- Lat stretch: 1 min each side

### Notes for All Templates

- **Progression**: Move to harder variations when you can complete all prescribed reps with good form
- **Rest Periods**: 2-3 min for strength work, 60-90 sec for volume work
- **Warm-Up**: Always include 5-10 min of joint circles and dynamic stretching
- **Skill Work**: Practice skill movements when fresh, at the beginning of sessions
- **Volume Management**: Reduce volume if recovery is compromised
- **Grip Care**: Maintain healthy calluses and use chalk as needed for bar work
