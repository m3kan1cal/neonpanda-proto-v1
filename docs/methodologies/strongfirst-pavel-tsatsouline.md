# StrongFirst (Pavel Tsatsouline)

## Overview

StrongFirst is a comprehensive strength training methodology developed by Pavel Tsatsouline, emphasizing minimalist training principles, kettlebell mastery, and the pursuit of strength as the foundation of all physical qualities. This system focuses on mastering fundamental movement patterns, developing maximum strength, and building resilient, capable human beings through time-tested training methods.

## Creator/Origin

StrongFirst was developed by Pavel Tsatsouline, a former Soviet Special Forces physical training instructor who introduced Russian kettlebell training to the Western world. Tsatsouline, a Master of Sports, built the methodology from Soviet/Russian military conditioning programs and sports science principles.

His philosophy that "strength has a greater purpose" emphasizes strength as the foundation for all other physical qualities. After emigrating to the United States, Tsatsouline founded StrongFirst in 2012 as an evolution of his previous work with the Russian Kettlebell Challenge (RKC). The organization certifies instructors in kettlebell, barbell, and bodyweight training, promoting minimalist, effective strength training principles worldwide.

## Key Principles

### 1. Strength First

- Maximum strength as the foundation of all physical qualities
- Strength endurance and power flow from absolute strength
- Focus on getting stronger rather than getting tired
- Quality over quantity in training

### 2. Minimalist Approach

- Few exercises performed with perfect technique
- Focus on fundamental movement patterns
- Elimination of unnecessary complexity
- Maximum return on training investment

### 3. Tension and Relaxation

- Mastery of muscular tension and relaxation
- Irradiation principle for strength development
- Breathing and tension coordination
- Efficiency through proper muscle activation

### 4. Progressive Overload

- Systematic increase in training demands
- Gradual progression over time
- Patience with the process
- Consistent improvement focus

### 5. Skill Development

- Strength as a skill to be practiced
- Technical mastery emphasis
- Movement quality over load
- Continuous refinement approach

## Programming Structure

### Daily Structure

- **Warm-up**: Movement preparation and activation (10-15 minutes)
- **Skill Practice**: Technical work and movement quality (15-20 minutes)

## WORKOUT TEMPLATES

**Programming Structure & Ratios:**

- **Minimalist Focus**: 2-4 exercises per session maximum
- **Quality Over Quantity**: Perfect reps only, never train to failure
- **Frequency**: 3-6 days per week depending on program
- **Session Duration**: 30-60 minutes (shorter, more frequent)

**Movement Patterns & Exercise Preferences:**

- **Kettlebell Primary**: Swing, get-up, snatch, clean & press
- **Barbell Focus**: Deadlift, press, squat (simple selection)
- **Bodyweight**: Pull-up, push-up, pistol squat
- **Movement Hierarchy**:
  1. Ballistics (swing, snatch) - power and conditioning
  2. Grinds (get-up, press, squat) - strength
  3. Carries and loaded mobility

**Time Domains & Workout Formats:**

- **Sessions**: 20-45 minutes of actual work
- **Rest**: Complete recovery between sets (typically 1-3 minutes)
- **Density Options**: Timed sets, ladders, EMOM formats
- **No Rushing**: Quality and freshness prioritized

**Scaling Approaches & Progression Methods:**

- **Ladder Progressions**: 1-2-3 reps, increasing rungs over time
- **Step Loading**: Increase reps before weight
- **Waving Loads**: Heavy/medium/light rotation
- **Quality Gate**: Only progress when movement is perfect

**Simple & Sinister Protocol (Daily Practice):**

```
Daily Template (5-6 days/week):
Warm-up (5-10 minutes):
- Goblet squats: 3 x 5
- Hip bridges: 3 x 10

Main Work:
ONE-ARM SWING: 10 x 10 (100 total)
TURKISH GET-UP: 10 total (5 each side)
Total time: 20-30 minutes
```

**Rite of Passage (Clean & Press):**

```
3-Day/Week:
Monday (Heavy): 1-2-3-4-5 x 5 ladders
Wednesday (Medium): 1-2-3-4 x 5 ladders
Friday (Light): 1-2-3 x 5 ladders
Pull-ups between ladders
```

**Power to the People (Barbell):**

```
Daily (5 days/week):
Deadlift: 2 x 5
Press: 2 x 5
Total time: 15-20 minutes
```
