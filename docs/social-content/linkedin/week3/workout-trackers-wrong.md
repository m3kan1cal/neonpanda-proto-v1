# Why Most Workout Trackers Get Abandoned

Most people abandon their fitness app within two weeks.

The reason? Friction.

Nobody wants to open an app, search for "Barbell Back Squat," find the right variation (trap bar? safety bar? vs high-bar?), input 5 sets, tap in the weight for each set, tap in the reps, confirm, repeat 20 times. That's not coaching. That's data entry.

**The real problem:** The app makes you think like a database instead of a lifter.

You don't think in sets and reps when you're in the gym. You think in workouts. "I did 5x5 back squat at 315, then some pull-ups. Hit it today." That's it. That's what actually happened.

**NeonPanda's natural language logging flips the script.**

Just tell your coach what you did. Talk to them like you'd talk to a friend:

"Did 5x5 back squat at 315, then some pull-ups"
"Ran 5 miles easy, felt good"
"Did Fran in 8:45"
"3x5 deadlift at 405, felt strong"

Your coach parses it, validates it, structures it, and logs it. Instantly. No friction. No thinking about what the app wants.

This is how fitness tracking should work — like a conversation, not a form.

The result? You actually stick with it.
